IoT-Based Plastic Degradation & Methane Utilization

Project Overview

This project focuses on using plastic-eating bacteria to break down plastic waste while capturing methane gas, which is utilized in biofuel cells. IoT sensors such as MQ-6 (Gas Sensor), DHT11 (Temperature & Humidity Sensor), and ESP CAM monitor the process, with data uploaded to ThingSpeak and processed using Django REST framework.

Working Process

  1. Plastic waste is placed in the bioreactor.
  2. *Ideonella sakaiensis* bacteria degrade the plastic into simpler organic compounds.
  3. Methanogenic bacteria convert these compounds into methane gas.
  4. MQ-6 Sensor detects methane concentration to measure gas production.
  5. DHT11 Sensor monitors temperature & humidity to ensure optimal bacterial activity.
  6. ESP CAM captures real-time images of plastic degradation to visually track progress.
  7. Data is uploaded to ThingSpeak and analyzed using Django REST framework for insights.
  8. Methane is purified and used in a biofuel cell, generating electricity as a renewable energy source.

Applications & Benefits

  • Reduces plastic waste through biological degradation.
  • Generates renewable energy by capturing and utilizing methane gas.
  • Real-time IoT monitoring optimizes the efficiency of the biodegradation process.
  • Scalable solution for waste management industries and renewable energy production.
